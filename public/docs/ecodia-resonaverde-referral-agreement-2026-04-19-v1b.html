Referral & Development Agreement

Between Ecodia Pty Ltd and Angelica Choppin (Resonaverde)

1. Parties

This Agreement is made between Ecodia Pty Ltd (ACN 693 123 278, ABN 89 693 123 278) of Sunshine Coast, Queensland, Australia (“Ecodia”), and Angelica Choppin, trading as Resonaverde (ABN to be inserted) of [address] (“Resonaverde”).

Each a “Party” and together the “Parties”.

2. Background

A. Ecodia provides AI, software and programming services.

B. Resonaverde has developed the concept for a community bartering platform known as CETIN and wishes to engage Ecodia to build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) of that platform.

C. Resonaverde has an established network of clients and contacts and is willing to refer potential clients to Ecodia for AI, software and programming services.

D. The Parties wish to formalise both the development engagement and the ongoing referral arrangement in a single Agreement.

3. CETIN MVP Development

3.1 Scope

Ecodia will design and develop an MVP of the CETIN community bartering application (the “MVP”), based on requirements to be jointly documented by the Parties in a separate scope document or statement of work. That scope document, once agreed in writing, forms part of this Agreement.

3.2 Price

The fixed price for the MVP is AUD $5,000 (plus GST) (the “MVP Fee”). The MVP Fee will not be invoiced to Resonaverde in cash. Instead, it will be satisfied by applying Commissions (as defined in clause 4) earned by Resonaverde against the MVP Fee, in accordance with clause 5.

3.3 Ownership of the MVP

Upon the MVP Fee being satisfied in full under clause 5, Ecodia assigns to Resonaverde all intellectual property rights in the MVP source code and deliverables, excluding any pre-existing tools, libraries, frameworks or know-how used by Ecodia (which remain Ecodia’s property and are licensed to Resonaverde on a perpetual, royalty-free basis to the extent required to use the MVP).

Prior to the MVP Fee being satisfied in full, Ecodia retains ownership of the MVP, but grants Resonaverde a non-exclusive licence to use and test the MVP for evaluation purposes.

3.4 Delivery and acceptance

Delivery timelines, milestones and acceptance criteria will be set out in the scope document referred to in clause 3.1. Ecodia will use reasonable commercial efforts to meet agreed timelines but is not liable for delays caused by matters outside its reasonable control, including delays in Resonaverde providing information, feedback or approvals.

4. Referral Arrangement

4.1 Exclusivity of referrals

Resonaverde agrees that, for the duration of this Agreement, any client or contact Resonaverde recommends to obtain AI, software or programming services will be referred exclusively to Ecodia. Resonaverde will not refer such clients to any other provider offering comparable services during the term of this Agreement.

4.2 Referral process

To qualify as a “Referred Client” under this Agreement, Resonaverde must submit a completed referral form (to be provided by Ecodia) to Ecodia before Ecodia first contacts or is contacted by that client in relation to services. The referral form must identify the client, the primary contact, and the nature of services anticipated.

A client will only be treated as a Referred Client once Ecodia has confirmed receipt of the referral form in writing (email sufficient). Clients who approach Ecodia independently, or who are introduced through channels other than a submitted and confirmed referral form, are not Referred Clients.

4.3 Commission structure

Subject to this Agreement, Ecodia will pay Resonaverde a commission (the “Commission”) on revenue actually received by Ecodia from Referred Clients, calculated on the GST-exclusive amount invoiced and paid, as follows:

  • Upfront fees and initial development / project work: 15%.
  • Ongoing support, retainer, maintenance, hosting, subscription or similar recurring services: 10%.

Commission on ongoing/recurring services is payable only for a period of 24 months from the date Ecodia first invoices that Referred Client for any service. After that 24-month period, no further Commission is payable in respect of that Referred Client, regardless of continued revenue.

No Commission is payable on: (a) reimbursed expenses or pass-through costs (e.g. third-party software licences, hosting paid to third parties, cloud infrastructure, domain fees); (b) GST or other taxes; (c) amounts refunded, charged back, or written off as bad debts; or (d) revenue received from clients who are not Referred Clients.

5. MVP Credit and Payment of Commissions

5.1 MVP Credit Account

Ecodia will maintain a running balance (the “MVP Credit Account”) recording Commissions earned by Resonaverde. Until the MVP Fee has been fully offset, all Commissions will be retained by Ecodia and applied against the MVP Fee, rather than paid to Resonaverde.

5.2 Payment after credit is satisfied

Once the cumulative Commissions earned equal or exceed the MVP Fee, the MVP Fee is taken to be satisfied. Any Commissions earned beyond that point will be paid to Resonaverde in cash, in accordance with clause 5.3.

5.3 Invoicing and payment

After the MVP Fee is satisfied, Resonaverde will invoice Ecodia monthly for Commissions accrued in the preceding calendar month. Ecodia will pay valid invoices within 14 days of receipt. If Resonaverde is registered for GST, Commission invoices must be valid tax invoices and Ecodia will pay the applicable GST in addition to the Commission.

5.4 Reporting

Ecodia will provide Resonaverde with a monthly statement showing: (a) revenue received from each Referred Client in the relevant period; (b) Commissions accrued; (c) the current balance of the MVP Credit Account; and (d) once applicable, Commissions payable in cash.

6. Term and Termination

6.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date it is signed by both Parties and continues until terminated in accordance with this clause 6.

6.2 Termination for convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by giving the other Party at least 30 days’ written notice.

6.3 Termination for breach or insolvency

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party: (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy it within 14 days after receiving written notice requiring remedy; or (b) becomes insolvent, enters into administration, liquidation, or a similar process.

6.4 Effect of termination on the MVP

If this Agreement is terminated before the MVP Fee is fully satisfied by Commissions:

  • Ecodia is not obliged to continue development of the MVP.
  • The MVP Credit Account is frozen, and any balance owing on the MVP Fee becomes immediately payable by Resonaverde to Ecodia in cash, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.
  • If Resonaverde pays the outstanding balance of the MVP Fee, Ecodia will deliver the MVP in its then-current state and assign intellectual property in accordance with clause 3.3.

6.5 Effect of termination on Commissions

On termination of this Agreement for any reason, Commissions cease entirely. No further Commissions are payable in respect of any Referred Client, including on any revenue received by Ecodia after the termination date, regardless of whether the Referred Client relationship continues. Commissions already accrued and unpaid as at the termination date (after satisfaction of the MVP Fee) remain payable to Resonaverde.

7. Mutual Obligations

Each Party will: (a) act in good faith in performance of this Agreement; (b) not make false or misleading representations about the other Party or its services; (c) comply with all applicable laws, including privacy and spam laws, when making or handling referrals; and (d) keep the other Party’s confidential information confidential, and use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

8. GST and Taxes

All amounts in this Agreement are expressed on a GST-exclusive basis. Where GST applies to a supply made under this Agreement, the recipient must pay the GST in addition to the stated amount, subject to receipt of a valid tax invoice. Each Party is responsible for its own income tax and other tax obligations.

9. Relationship of the Parties

The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment or fiduciary relationship between them. Neither Party may hold itself out as having authority to bind the other.

10. Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither Party is liable to the other for indirect, consequential, special or incidental loss, or loss of profit, revenue, goodwill, business opportunity or data. Each Party’s total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement (other than for unpaid Commissions or unpaid MVP Fee) is limited to the total amount of the MVP Fee.

Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits any liability that cannot lawfully be excluded or limited, including under the Australian Consumer Law.

11.1 Entire agreement

11.2 Variation

Any variation must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

11.4 Governing law

11.5 Notices

Notices under this Agreement must be in writing and may be given by email to the addresses notified by the Parties from time to time.

11.6 Counterparts

This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including by electronic signature, each of which is deemed an original and all of which together form one instrument.
