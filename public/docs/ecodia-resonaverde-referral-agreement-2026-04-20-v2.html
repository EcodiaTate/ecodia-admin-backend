1. Parties

This Agreement is made between Ecodia Pty Ltd (ABN 86 688 476 082) of Sunshine Coast, Queensland, Australia ("Ecodia"), and Angelica Choppin, trading as Resonaverde (ABN 65 261 891 077) of 15 Fairy Wren Court, Beerwah QLD 4519 ("Resonaverde").

Each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

2. Background

A. Ecodia provides AI, software and programming services (the "Services"), described at a high level in Appendix A.

B. Resonaverde has an established network of clients and contacts and is willing to refer potential clients to Ecodia for the Services.

C. The Parties wish to formalise this referral arrangement on the terms set out below.

D. Any separate engagement between the Parties (including any future development of the CETN / Circular Economy Tech Network platform) will be governed by its own scope document and statement of work, and is not covered by this Agreement.

3. Referral Arrangement

3.1 Right of First Refusal

Where Resonaverde identifies a client or contact who is seeking AI, software or programming services, Resonaverde will first offer Ecodia a right of first refusal in respect of that opportunity, by notifying Ecodia in accordance with clause 3.2.

Ecodia will respond in writing within 5 business days indicating whether it accepts the referral. If Ecodia declines, does not respond within that period, or indicates that the scope is outside its capability, Resonaverde is free to refer that client to any other provider, and no Commission is payable under this Agreement in respect of that client.

Resonaverde is not otherwise restricted from referring clients to, or recommending, any other provider for services that fall outside the scope described in Appendix A.

3.2 Referral process

To qualify as a "Referred Client" under this Agreement, Resonaverde must submit a referral notice (by email is sufficient) to Ecodia before Ecodia first contacts, or is contacted by, that client in relation to the Services. The referral notice must identify the client, the primary contact, and the nature of services anticipated.

A client will only be treated as a Referred Client once Ecodia has confirmed receipt of the referral notice in writing (email sufficient) and has accepted the referral under clause 3.1. Clients who approach Ecodia independently, or who are introduced through channels other than a submitted and confirmed referral notice, are not Referred Clients.

3.3 Commission structure

Ecodia will pay Resonaverde a commission (the "Commission") on revenue actually received by Ecodia from Referred Clients, calculated on the GST-exclusive amount invoiced and paid, as follows:

  • Upfront fees and initial development / project work: 15%.
  • Ongoing support, retainer, maintenance, hosting, subscription or similar recurring services: 10%.

For clarity, Commission applies to all AI, software and programming services invoiced by Ecodia to a Referred Client within 24 months of Ecodia's first invoice to that client, including ad hoc work, additional projects, support, and recurring services falling within the above categories. After that 24-month period, no further Commission is payable in respect of that Referred Client, regardless of continued revenue.

No Commission is payable on: (a) reimbursed expenses or pass-through costs (e.g. third-party software licences, hosting paid to third parties, cloud infrastructure, domain fees); (b) GST or other taxes; (c) amounts refunded, charged back, or written off as bad debts; or (d) revenue received from clients who are not Referred Clients.

4. Invoicing, Payment and Reporting

4.1 Monthly cycle

Within 5 business days of the end of each calendar month, Ecodia will provide Resonaverde with a statement showing: (a) revenue received from each Referred Client during the prior month; (b) Commissions accrued; (c) any Commissions carried forward from prior months; and (d) total Commission payable for the month.

4.2 Invoicing and payment

Following receipt of the monthly statement, Resonaverde will invoice Ecodia for the total Commission payable. Ecodia will pay valid invoices within 14 days of receipt. If Resonaverde is registered for GST, Commission invoices must be valid tax invoices and Ecodia will pay the applicable GST in addition to the Commission.

4.3 Audit right

Resonaverde may, no more than once per calendar quarter, request a reasonable breakdown of invoices issued to any Referred Client for the purpose of verifying Commission calculations. Ecodia will respond within 10 business days. If a material error in Ecodia's favour (greater than 5% of Commission payable for the relevant period) is identified, Ecodia will bear the reasonable cost of the audit and promptly pay the shortfall; otherwise the cost of preparing the breakdown is borne by Resonaverde.

5. Term and Termination

5.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the date it is signed by both Parties and continues until terminated in accordance with this clause 5.

5.2 Termination for convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by giving the other Party at least 30 days' written notice.

5.3 Termination for breach or insolvency

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party: (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy it within 14 days after receiving written notice requiring remedy; or (b) becomes insolvent, enters into administration, liquidation, or a similar process.

5.4 Effect of termination on Commissions

On termination, Commissions are treated as follows:

  • Termination for convenience, or by Resonaverde for Ecodia's material breach: Commissions continue to be payable in respect of Referred Clients introduced before the termination date, on revenue received by Ecodia during a tail period of 6 months from the termination date, subject to the 24-month per-client cap in clause 3.3.
  • Termination by Ecodia for Resonaverde's material breach, or by either Party for the other's insolvency: Commissions cease on the termination date. No further Commission is payable in respect of any Referred Client, including on revenue received by Ecodia after termination.
  • Commissions accrued and unpaid as at the termination date remain payable in accordance with clause 4.

6. Mutual Obligations

Each Party will: (a) act in good faith in performance of this Agreement; (b) not make false or misleading representations about the other Party or its services; (c) comply with all applicable laws, including privacy and spam laws, when making or handling referrals; and (d) keep the other Party's confidential information confidential, and use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

7. GST and Taxes

All amounts in this Agreement are expressed on a GST-exclusive basis. Where GST applies to a supply made under this Agreement, the recipient must pay the GST in addition to the stated amount, subject to receipt of a valid tax invoice. Each Party is responsible for its own income tax and other tax obligations.

8. Relationship of the Parties

The Parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment or fiduciary relationship between them. Neither Party may hold itself out as having authority to bind the other.

9. Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, neither Party is liable to the other for indirect, consequential, special or incidental loss, or loss of profit, revenue, goodwill, business opportunity or data.

Each Party's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement (other than for unpaid Commissions or amounts expressly payable under this Agreement) is limited to the greater of: (a) the total Commissions paid or payable under this Agreement in the 12 months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim; or (b) AUD $1,000.

Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits any liability that cannot lawfully be excluded or limited, including under the Australian Consumer Law.

Ecodia will, where helpful, recommend preferred partners for these services on request.
