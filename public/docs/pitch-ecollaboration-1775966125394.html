01   The Problem

What's holding you back

Community conservation on the Sunshine Coast runs on volunteer goodwill and overstretched coordinators. Ecollaboration manages dozens of active projects, hundreds of volunteers, and thousands of native plantings - but the tools have not kept pace with the ambition.
●

Event scheduling and volunteer sign-up managed through email and social media

●

No systematic tracking of native plantings, survival rates, or ecological outcomes

●

Volunteer engagement drops off after first event due to lack of follow-up

●

Grant reporting requires manual collation of data from multiple project sites

02   The Solution

What we'll build together

A community conservation management platform. One place for volunteers to discover projects, sign up, track their planting impact, and stay connected to the Ecollaboration community. One dashboard for coordinators to manage everything.
01

Project discovery hub with upcoming events, location maps, and instant sign-up

02

Planting tracker - log species planted, GPS location, and monitor survival rates over time

03

Volunteer impact dashboard - trees planted, hours contributed, sites restored

04

Coordinator tools for event management, volunteer communication, and task assignment

05

Photo timeline showing restoration progress at each project site

06

Automated grant reporting with planting numbers, volunteer hours, and ecological outcomes

07

Real-time impact measurement and reporting

Impact measurement isn't an add-on. It's built into every feature. Every event, every volunteer hour, every action is tracked and reportable.
