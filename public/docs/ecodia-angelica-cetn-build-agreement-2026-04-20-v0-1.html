CETN MVP Build Agreement

Between Ecodia Pty Ltd and Angelica Choppin — v0.1, 20 April 2026

1. Parties

This Agreement is made between Ecodia Pty Ltd (ABN 86 688 476 082) of Sunshine Coast, Queensland, Australia ("Ecodia"), and Angelica Choppin (ABN 65 261 891 077) of 15 Fairy Wren Court, Beerwah QLD 4519 ("Client").

Each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

2. Background

A. The Client is the proponent of the Circular Economy Tech Network ("CETN"), a platform concept described in the Scope Document referred to in clause 3.

B. The Client wishes to engage Ecodia to design, build and deliver a Minimum Viable Product (the "MVP") implementing the CETN concept.

C. Ecodia is willing to build the MVP on a founder-friendly, discounted build fee, in exchange for retaining ownership of the resulting software and licensing it to the Client on the terms set out in this Agreement.

D. This Agreement is independent of, and does not alter, the Referral Agreement between Ecodia and Angelica Choppin (Resonaverde) dated April 2026.

3. Scope

The work to be performed by Ecodia under this Agreement (the "Work") is described in the document titled "CETN MVP Scope v0.1" ("Scope Document"), which is incorporated into this Agreement by reference.

The Scope Document may be updated by written agreement of the Parties. Changes that materially expand the Work (as reasonably determined by Ecodia) require a written change order and may attract additional fees at Ecodia's then-current rates.

One round of reasonable revisions is included within the Build Fee. Further rounds of revision are billable at Ecodia's hourly rate (currently AUD 120 + GST per hour) or under a separate maintenance retainer.

4. Fees, Payment and Triggers

4.1 Build Fee

The total build fee is AUD 5,000 + GST (the "Build Fee"), payable as follows:

  • Deposit: AUD 500 + GST on signing of this Agreement. Deposit is non-refundable once kickoff begins and is credited toward the Build Fee.
  • Deferred balance: AUD 4,500 + GST, payable on the earliest to occur of the following triggers:
    1. 12 months from the date Ecodia certifies the MVP as delivered under clause 9.1; or
    2. the date on which the Client receives cumulative grant, investment, or equivalent third-party funding of AUD 10,000 or more in respect of CETN (the Client will notify Ecodia in writing within 10 business days of receiving any such funding); or
    3. such earlier date as the Client elects in writing under clause 4.2.

4.2 Optional offset via referral commissions

The Client may, at any time and by written notice to Ecodia, elect to direct all or part of any commissions accrued or payable to her under the Referral Agreement toward the Deferred Balance under clause 4.1. Ecodia will apply the offset against the Deferred Balance in the order in which commissions accrue. This clause is a voluntary payment mechanism only and does not create any obligation on Ecodia to refer business, or on the Client to accept offset, under the Referral Agreement.

4.3 Monthly Licence Fee

From the date that is 60 days after the MVP is certified as delivered under clause 9.1 (the "Licence Start Date"), the Client will pay Ecodia a monthly licence fee (the "Licence Fee") of AUD 199 + GST per month, payable monthly in advance by direct debit or card on file.

If the Client elects to prepay 12 months of Licence Fee in advance, the prepaid rate is AUD 1,200 + GST per annum (approximately AUD 100 + GST per month equivalent), provided the full amount is received before the start of the prepaid period.

The Licence Fee is reviewed annually and may be increased by Ecodia by up to CPI (Australia) plus 3 percentage points per year on 30 days written notice.

4.4 What the Licence Fee covers

The Licence Fee covers:

  • Operating the MVP on Ecodia's hosting infrastructure (Vercel and Supabase or equivalent);
  • Third-party hosting costs up to AUD 20 + GST per month pass-through; costs above that threshold are billable as pre-approved Hosting Overages;
  • Security patches and minor dependency updates;
  • Minor bug fixes that do not require more than 1 hour of Ecodia engineering time per calendar month (cumulative);
  • Response to support queries on a best-efforts basis during Australian business hours.

Feature work, design changes, content updates, integrations, performance optimisations, or material bug investigations beyond the covered scope are not included and are billable separately, at Ecodia's then-current rates or under a separate retainer.

4.5 GST

All amounts in this Agreement are exclusive of GST unless stated otherwise. Where GST is payable on a taxable supply under this Agreement, the recipient will pay an additional amount equal to the GST on provision of a valid tax invoice.

5. Operating Licence

5.1 Grant

Subject to the Client's compliance with this Agreement (including payment of the Licence Fee when it falls due), Ecodia grants the Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable licence to use the MVP for the sole purpose of operating the CETN platform in the ordinary course of the Client's business (the "Operating Licence").

5.2 Scope of use

The Operating Licence permits the Client to access, operate, and commercialise the MVP (including charging end users, accepting memberships, and processing transactions through the platform). The Operating Licence does not permit the Client to:

  1. copy, fork, re-host, sublicense, sell, or distribute the MVP code or any substantial portion of it;
  2. create derivative works of the MVP outside of Ecodia's engagement;
  3. use the MVP to build competing platforms for third parties; or
  4. remove or alter any notices or attributions in the MVP.

5.3 Suspension for non-payment

If the Client is in arrears on the Licence Fee for 60 days or more, Ecodia may, on 10 business days written notice, suspend the Operating Licence and the operation of the MVP. The Operating Licence is automatically reinstated upon the Client curing the arrears in full, including any reactivation fee not exceeding AUD 200 + GST.

5.4 No buyout in this Agreement

This Agreement does not provide for the sale or assignment of the intellectual property in the MVP to the Client. The Parties may, in future, negotiate a separate IP buyout at arm's-length terms; no terms for any such buyout are pre-agreed under this Agreement.

6. Intellectual Property

6.1 Ownership

All right, title and interest (including copyright and all other intellectual property rights) in and to the MVP and all code, designs, configurations, documentation, and deliverables produced by Ecodia under this Agreement are, and remain, owned by Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd (or its nominee), an associated entity of Ecodia. The Parties acknowledge that Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd licenses the relevant tooling and components to Ecodia for the purpose of performing the Work.

6.2 Pre-existing Ecodia materials

Any pre-existing tools, libraries, templates, frameworks, and know-how used by Ecodia in the Work remain the sole property of Ecodia and/or Ecodia Labs Pty Ltd. The Operating Licence extends to the Client's permitted use of these materials as embedded in the MVP.

6.3 Client materials

Content, branding, logos, copy, images, and other materials provided by the Client for incorporation into the MVP remain the Client's property. The Client grants Ecodia a non-exclusive licence to use those materials as reasonably required to build, host and operate the MVP.

6.4 Client data

As between the Parties, the Client owns all user and business data generated through the operation of the MVP ("Client Data"). Ecodia is a processor of Client Data for the purpose of operating the MVP and will handle it in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles and the CETN privacy policy (to be agreed).

6.5 Portfolio rights

Ecodia may reference the Work in its portfolio, marketing, and case studies, and may describe the MVP at a general level, provided that no confidential Client Data or commercially sensitive information is disclosed.

7. Hosting, Data, and Operations

The MVP will be hosted on Ecodia-controlled infrastructure. The Client acknowledges that the MVP depends on third-party services (including Vercel, Supabase, and payment providers) that may change their terms or pricing from time to time.

On termination of this Agreement under clause 9 (other than for Client's uncured material breach), Ecodia will, at the Client's request and subject to reasonable assistance from the Client, provide a one-off export of Client Data in a commercially reasonable format. Data export requests during the term of the Agreement are provided on the same basis, without additional charge more than twice per calendar year.

8. Warranty

Ecodia warrants that for a period of 30 days from the date of delivery certification under clause 9.1, the MVP will materially conform to the Scope Document. Ecodia will fix, at no additional charge, any material defects the Client reports during this warranty period.

Outside this 30-day period, bug fixes are handled under the Licence Fee cover in clause 4.4, under a separate paid retainer, or on an hourly billable basis.

Except as expressly set out in this Agreement, the MVP and the Services are provided on an "as-is" basis. All other warranties, express or implied, are excluded to the maximum extent permitted by law.

9. Term and Termination

9.1 Delivery

Ecodia will certify the MVP as "delivered" when it materially conforms to the Scope Document and has been deployed to a production environment accessible to the Client. The Client will have 7 business days to review and either accept delivery or submit a written list of material defects with reference to the Scope Document. If no written response is received within 7 business days, delivery is deemed accepted.

9.2 Term

This Agreement commences on the date it is signed by both Parties and continues until terminated in accordance with this clause.

9.3 Termination for convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement on 30 days written notice.

9.4 Termination for cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party:

  1. commits a material breach and does not cure it within 14 days of written notice; or
  2. becomes insolvent, enters administration, or has a receiver appointed.

9.5 Effect of termination

On termination:

  • The Operating Licence ends and the Client will cease use of the MVP.
  • Any Deferred Balance under clause 4.1 that has been triggered and remains unpaid is immediately due and payable; any Deferred Balance that has not yet been triggered falls away unless termination is for the Client's uncured material breach, in which case it becomes immediately due.
  • Accrued Licence Fees up to the effective date of termination remain payable; prepaid Licence Fees covering periods after the effective date are refundable pro rata, except where termination is for the Client's uncured material breach.
  • Ecodia retains all intellectual property rights in the MVP. The Client retains Client Data and any content or materials it provided.

10. Confidentiality

Each Party will keep the other's confidential information confidential and use it only for the purposes of performing this Agreement. This obligation does not apply to information that is public, independently developed, or required to be disclosed by law.

11. Limitation of Liability

Neither Party is liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, or special loss, including loss of profits, loss of business opportunity, or loss of goodwill.

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Ecodia's aggregate liability to the Client arising out of or in connection with this Agreement is capped at the total amount actually paid by the Client to Ecodia under this Agreement in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the liability, with a minimum of AUD 1,000. This cap does not apply to liabilities that cannot be excluded or capped at law.

12. General

12.1 Governing law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of Queensland, Australia. Each Party submits to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Queensland and the Federal Court of Australia sitting in Queensland.

12.2 Notices

Notices under this Agreement must be in writing and sent by email to the addresses nominated by the Parties from time to time. Notice is taken to be received on the next business day after sending.

12.3 Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld. Consent is deemed given for assignment to a related body corporate under common control, provided written notice is given.

12.4 Entire agreement

This Agreement (together with the Scope Document) is the entire agreement between the Parties in respect of the MVP build and supersedes all prior discussions, proposals, and understandings on that subject. For clarity, this Agreement does not alter the separate Referral Agreement between the Parties.

12.5 Variation

Any variation must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.6 Severability

If any clause is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remainder of this Agreement continues in force.
