05
What the tech actually is

The platform, briefly

A working, multi-tenant operating layer for collective-care organisations. Owned by Ecodia. Built for the shape of org Yarn and Yield is, not for the brand of any specific deployment.

Capabilities, in plain terms

  • Members, branches, working bees, events, sign-ups, check-ins, post-event sharing
  • Impact tracking: trees planted, kilograms of rubbish, attendees, hours contributed. Reverse-sync from spreadsheet operational tools to canonical data the org owns.
  • Per-org configuration. Yarn and Yield's three-branch model is a configuration, not a fork.
  • Per-tenant deployment. Your data stays in your tenant. We do not co-mingle.

What it is not

  • Not a custom build for one org. The platform exists. Per-tenant work is configuration, with code touched only when the platform itself improves.
  • Not a re-skin of someone else's brand. Yarn and Yield's deployment would carry Yarn and Yield's identity end to end.

The actual ask

Tier 1: ninety days, contributed in kind

No invoice. No platform fee. No commitment past 90 days.
  • Tate joins as a member-collaborator and contributes to working bees in person
  • We run one event on Nalbo within 90 days, using a Yarn and Yield staging of our platform. A working bee, a tree-planting day, or a Practice-branch session: whatever the board picks.

The point of Tier 1 is not the event. It is to earn the right to the next conversation. No ongoing commitment from you.

Past the first ask

Tier 3 - hinted, only if you want to open the door

Yarn and Yield's stated long arc is a multi-property CLT network across Australia. The technology and governance layer for that network does not exist yet. We are building components of it for ourselves. If Yarn and Yield ever decides it wants a technology and AI partner for the multi-property CLT, we would like to be the first conversation. This is a five-to-ten year horizon. We mention it once.

Both tiers are gated on Tier 1 earning the right to a Tier 2 conversation. We are not asking you to commit to anything past Tier 1 today.

Saying it first

Risks we acknowledge

  • "We have a software engineer on the board already." Yes. We are not pitching a custom build that competes with that role. The platform exists. Your engineer keeps building whatever sits on top, alongside, or around it.
  • "We are not yet ready for a public technology partner." We respect that. Tier 1 has nothing public-facing. One event on Nalbo, internal to your members. Low risk for you.
  • "Why not just pay you to do it?" Because reciprocity is more honest than vendor-client at this stage. If we are still doing this in a year, both sides will have evidence to set commercial terms on. Setting them today would be guessing.
  • "You are a small team." Yes. We are also two legal entities, a working platform, and a 24/7 algorithmic operator. Capacity is not the bottleneck the size suggests.
