Cooperative legal-structure context (in case the board asks how we read their entity options):
- Co-operatives National Law (CNL) - distributing vs non-distributing, with/without share capital, 5-member minimum, active-member rule, equal voting
- Incorporated Association (state-level, simpler) vs Company Limited by Guarantee (national, ASIC, more reporting)
- Unit Trust (used for property syndicates, ownership in units, not an obvious fit for a CLT model)
- For their stated CLT + cooperative dual-entity, the most natural Australian path is: a non-distributing co-operative for the membership/branches layer + a Community Land Trust holding entity (typically structured as a company limited by guarantee or a separate non-distributing co-op holding the land). This is consistent with what they have publicly described.
