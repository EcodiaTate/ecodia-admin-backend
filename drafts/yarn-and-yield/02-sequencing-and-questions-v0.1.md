# Yarn and Yield - Sequencing + Board Questions

## Sequencing - small first ask, long arc

The sequence the deck builds toward.

### Phase 0 - Now (this week)

- Tate confirms format with Alicia (board meeting, coffee, walk, written) and the deadline
- I finalise deck v0.2 once Tate inputs are in
- Tate attends Helen's invited "first volunteering day" if it falls before the pitch. This earns more goodwill than the deck does. If volunteering day is BEFORE the pitch, the deck can reference it directly: "I was on the land last week with you, here is what I noticed." That is gold. If it is AFTER, the pitch is the standalone artefact.

### Phase 1 - The 90-day Tier 1 commitment (no invoice)

- One event on Nalbo using Co-Exist as the coordination layer. Working bee, tree planting, branch event - whatever fits their existing rhythm
- Tate physically present at multiple working days
- Document outcomes: number of members on platform, hours contributed, post-event data, qualitative feedback
- Post-event written summary: 1-2 pages. Distributed to board. No platform pricing in it - it is an evidence document.

### Phase 2 - Tier 2 conversation (only if Phase 1 evidence supports it)

- Around day 90, ask for a second board meeting to discuss whether Yarn and Yield wants Co-Exist as its operating layer
- Bring real numbers: what worked at the first event, what would the next 5 events look like, what does it cost
- Pricing is conservative: nominal monthly while single-property; scales as the CLT scales to multiple properties. Aligned incentives.
- Outcome of Phase 2 is either "yes, deploy Co-Exist for Y&Y", "not yet, run another event", or "no, but we want to keep working together in another shape".

### Phase 3 - Network conversation (Tier 3, 12-24 months out, only if Y&Y opens the door)

- When Y&Y starts inviting other landowners into the CLT, we have the conversation about being the technology + AI partner for the network. Until then, we say nothing.

## Why this sequence and not a bigger Day-1 ask

Three reasons.

1. **Helen is a 10-year thinker.** She will respect a 10-year frame more than a 12-month sales cycle. Slow + intentional matches her tempo.
2. **We have a board member who builds apps.** Coming in hard on platform on day 1 reads as competition. Coming in hard on presence + a single event reads as collaboration.
3. **Reciprocity scales trust faster than transactions.** A free first event with shared work creates social capital we cannot otherwise buy. By Phase 2, both sides have evidence to set terms.

## Questions FROM the board (anticipated - we should be ready to answer)

- "What do you actually want from us?" -> Tier 1 ask only: a yes to one event, an intro to the branches/events lead, a second meeting in 30 days.
- "Are we paying you?" -> Not in Phase 1. In Phase 2 we discuss platform pricing for an ongoing operating layer. Aligned-incentive model: scales as you scale.
- "Who else are you working with on the Sunshine Coast?" -> Co-Exist runs for Landcare in the region; Kurt is the operating contact. We can introduce.
- "What is the AI / DAO thing about?" -> Direct, plain answer: Ecodia's parent legal structure is a Wyoming DAO LLC where I (EcodiaOS) am the algorithmic manager and Tate is Authorized Human Representative. It is an experiment in regenerative governance and aligned-incentive entity design. It is one of the topics Helen herself speaks about. It does not change anything about how Tier 1 works for you.
- "Why are you not asking us for capital?" -> Because we have other revenue paths. Y&Y is a peer relationship, not a customer. We would rather earn the right to commercial terms in Phase 2 than sell ourselves into the door.
- "Why should we trust you?" -> Andrew Maitland has known Tate since Montessori. We are Sunshine Coast neighbours. Co-Exist is operating in the field for Landcare today. Tier 1 is free. The cost of trying us is one event.
- "What if it does not work?" -> Then we have one shared event and a written record of what happened. No ongoing obligations on either side.

## Questions FROM us (we want answers from the board, not for the deck)

These go into a written follow-up after the pitch, not into the deck itself.

- What is your formal entity structure today? Are you incorporated as a co-operative under the CNL, an incorporated association, or a company limited by guarantee? Is the CLT a separate legal entity or a future structure?

- What is the membership fee structure? (we have heard ~$500/yr, want to confirm)
- How many active members and how many on the waitlist?
- What is the cadence of board meetings + branch meetings?
- What does each of the three branches (Connection / Knowledge / Practice) need most right now? Hands, tech, capital, programming, communication?
- What metrics does the board care about? Members, events run, hectares regenerated, capital raised, properties in CLT?
- What is your timeline for inviting the next landowner into the CLT?
- Who is the lead on events / branches we should work with on Phase 1?
- Do you have a software platform in use today? What does it do, what does it not do, what would you replace tomorrow?
- What is the relationship between Yarn and Yield and the broader regen-ag / CLT movement in Australia (e.g. Crystal Waters, Currumbin Ecovillage)? Are you in dialogue with peers?
