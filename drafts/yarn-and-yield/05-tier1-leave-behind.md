## Tier 1, in five bullets

1. **One event on Nalbo within 90 days.** A working bee, tree-planting day, or Practice-branch session. Yarn and Yield picks the shape. Ecodia runs the digital coordination layer (sign-ups, check-in, impact stats, post-event sharing). At the end, Yarn and Yield owns the data, the members on platform, and a working demo.

2. **Tate joins as a member-collaborator and shows up in person.** Working bees included. We are present, not remote. Sunshine Coast neighbours.

3. **A working tenant of our platform, configured to your three-branch model.** Connection, Knowledge, Practice. Per-tenant, your data stays in your tenant. Identity end-to-end is yours.

4. **A short written debrief inside 14 days of the event.** What worked, what did not, what Yarn and Yield could do with it next. Honest. Useful whether you continue with us or not.

5. **A second meeting in 30 days after the event.** Compare notes. Decide jointly whether there is a Tier 2 conversation worth having.

## What it costs you

**Zero dollars. No invoice. No platform fee. No commitment past 90 days.**

The only thing we ask: an introduction to whichever board member runs the events and branches calendar, so we can scope the first session jointly.

## Why we are doing it for free

Reciprocity is more honest than vendor-client at this stage. We are Sunshine Coast neighbours, vouched for by Andrew Maitland, with the same regenerative thesis as Yarn and Yield. If we are still doing this in a year, both sides will have evidence to set commercial terms on. Setting them today would be guessing.
