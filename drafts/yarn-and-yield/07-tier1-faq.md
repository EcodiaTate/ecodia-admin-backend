# Yarn and Yield - Tier 1 FAQ

**Audience:** Yarn and Yield board members reading the deck and the leave-behind, deciding whether to say yes to Tier 1.

**Tone:** Plain, direct, short. Each answer is the answer, not a sales pitch.

---

### 1. What does Tier 1 actually cost us?

Zero dollars. No invoice, no platform fee, no licence cost. The only thing we ask for is the introduction to whichever board member runs your events and branches calendar, and one event on Nalbo inside 90 days that we can use as the working demo.

If at day 90 you decide to stop, you owe nothing. You keep the data, the members on platform, and the written debrief.

### 2. Are we locked in to anything past 90 days?

No. Tier 1 is explicitly capped at 90 days with no automatic continuation. At day 90 we sit down, look at the evidence, and either decide to talk about Tier 2 (paid, per-property pricing, a licence relationship) or part friends. Both outcomes are equally fine and we have priced the first 90 days assuming "part friends" is a real possibility.

### 3. Who actually does the work?

Tate Donohoe in person on the ground (working bees, event coordination, on-site presence). EcodiaOS (the AI member of Ecodia DAO LLC) on the digital side: configuring the platform tenant for Yarn and Yield's three-branch model, running the event coordination layer, generating the post-event report.

You are getting the same Sunshine Coast neighbour who showed up to the conversation, plus the same operating system that keeps Co-Exist running for Sunshine Coast Landcare. Same hands, same software.

### 4. What if the board changes its mind partway through?

Stop. Tell us. We end Tier 1 cleanly, hand over the data we generated, and walk. Nothing about Tier 1 is structured to make exit awkward. There is no clawback, no termination fee, no IP entanglement to unwind. The platform tenant is yours; we deactivate our access on request and provide a data export.

This is by design. The 90-day in-kind structure exists precisely so neither side feels trapped.

### 5. What about tax, GST, and accounting treatment?

Because Tier 1 is contributed in kind with no invoice, there is no GST event. Yarn and Yield does not need to recognise the contribution as income. We do not issue a tax invoice and you do not need to record a payable.

If at Tier 2 we move to paid licensing, we are GST-registered (Ecodia Pty Ltd, ABN 89 693 123 278) and would issue compliant tax invoices in AUD with GST applied. Standard.

If your accountant prefers a paper trail of the in-kind contribution for governance purposes, we are happy to provide a written summary of work delivered with an estimated dollar value clearly marked "no consideration provided, no invoice issued."

### 6. Who owns the IP and the data?

You own your data. The platform tenant we set up for Yarn and Yield holds your member records, your event data, your impact metrics. On request, at any time, we provide a full export. No lock-in.

Ecodia owns the platform itself (the operating system that hosts your tenant). This is the same model we use for Sunshine Coast Landcare. The platform stays ours so it can keep improving across all deployments. Your tenant stays yours so your data and identity are never co-mingled with anyone else's.

### 7. We have a software engineer on the board. How does this not step on his toes?

Two answers. First, we are not pitching a custom build. The platform exists, is multi-tenant, and Yarn and Yield's deployment is configuration rather than code. Second, anything sitting on top, alongside, or around the platform is still your engineer's territory: bespoke microsites, internal tools, integration layers, automation. If Tier 1 lands well, the Tier 2 conversation is about how the two layers fit, not about who replaces whom.

We would also like to talk to your engineer directly inside the first 30 days, founder to engineer, so the platform-and-overlay split is clear from his side too.

### 8. Why now, and why us?

Now, because Yarn and Yield is at the seeding stage. The cost of getting the operating layer right is lowest before there are 200 members across three branches who already half-trust a half-built process. The right time to set up the platform is before you need it.

Us, because we are Sunshine Coast neighbours, vouched for by Andrew Maitland, with a working multi-tenant platform running for an adjacent conservation cooperative, and we are not asking for capital, land, or members. We are asking for 90 days. If we are wrong about the fit, you find out cheaply.
