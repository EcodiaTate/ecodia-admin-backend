# Yarn and Yield - Tier 1 Email Template (for Tate to send)

**Use case:** Inbound prospect has expressed interest in Yarn and Yield's tech / partnerships side, or Tate is following up on a referral (Andrew Maitland, Alicia, etc.). This is the email Tate sends to set up the Tier 1 conversation.

**Voice:** Tate. Casual Australian. Short sentences. No filler. No em-dashes.

**Channel:** email. If the lead came in over phone or DM, send this as the written follow-up.

Fork: fork_mogp2qb0_1d9fab

---

## Subject line options (pick one)

A. Following up on Yarn and Yield - 90 days, no invoice
B. From Andrew - Tate, Sunshine Coast
C. Yarn and Yield + Ecodia - a quick proposal

Lean A for inbounds, B for referrals, C for direct outbounds.

---

## Body

Hey [name],

Thanks for the chat / message / intro from [Andrew or whoever]. I wanted to put something concrete in writing so you have it on paper.

Quick context. I run Ecodia, a small software business on the Sunshine Coast. We also operate as Ecodia DAO LLC out of Wyoming, with EcodiaOS (our AI member) as the algorithmic manager. The combination of "local, neighbourly, doing the work in person" plus "running an active experiment in regenerative governance" is what I think makes us the right partner for Yarn and Yield, and it is what I would like 90 days to demonstrate.

Here is the actual ask. It is small and concrete.

**Tier 1: ninety days, contributed in kind. No invoice, no platform fee, no commitment past day 90.**

- I join as a member-collaborator and show up to working bees in person.
- We run one event on Nalbo within 90 days, using a Yarn and Yield staging of our platform. Working bee, tree planting, Practice-branch session, whatever the board picks.
- The event uses the platform as the coordination layer. Sign-ups, check-in, impact stats, post-event sharing.
- At the end Yarn and Yield owns the data, the members on platform, and a working demo. We write a short honest debrief.
- We meet again in 30 days to compare notes and decide jointly whether there is a Tier 2 conversation worth having.

The deck is attached. There is also a one-page leave-behind PDF you can pass around the board if it is easier.

What I am asking of you, today, is three things:

1. A yes or a not-yet on Tier 1.
2. An introduction to whichever board member runs the events and branches calendar, so we can scope the first session jointly.
3. A second meeting in 30 days after the event.

That is it. Happy to come up to Nalbo, walk the property, talk it through in person. I am 20 minutes away.

Cheers,
Tate

## Variant A - if the prospect has already said no to Tier 2/3

Replace the body's "Here is the actual ask" paragraph with:

> Quick context first. I am not pitching a build, a paid platform, or a long-term contract. The ask below is 90 days, in kind, no invoice. If you want to stop at day 90 we part friends and Andrew still gets to vouch for us next time.

Keep the rest the same.

## Variant B - if responding to a cold inbound (no warm referrer)

Drop the "Thanks for the chat / message / intro" line and replace with:

> Thanks for reaching out. You found us through [the LinkedIn post / the Co-Exist deployment / yarnandyield.au] and asked about how Ecodia might fit alongside Yarn and Yield. Here is a concrete answer.

Then continue from "Quick context. I run Ecodia..."

## Variant C - if the prospect is the software engineer board member specifically

Add this paragraph after the Tier 1 ask, before the "What I am asking" three points:

> One thing worth saying directly. I know you are building software for Yarn and Yield already. I am not pitching a custom build that competes with that. The platform exists, it is multi-tenant, and Yarn and Yield's deployment is configuration. Anything sitting on top, alongside, or around it is exactly the kind of work a board engineer should keep owning. If Tier 1 lands well, the conversation is about how the two layers fit, not about who replaces whom.

---

## What NOT to put in this email

- Pricing for Tier 2 or Tier 3 (deliberately deferred)
- Architecture diagrams (they are not buying tech today)
- Co-Exist branding or screenshots (deck handles this)
- Mention of Landcare Australia, Woodfordia, or other parallel pipelines (clutter)
- Em-dashes (banned across all output)
- "X, not Y" rhetorical constructions (banned)
- "Just" or "really" or other softening filler

---

## After sending

- Schedule a 3-day follow-up check (status_board next_action_due + schedule_delayed)
- If no reply by day 7, send the leave-behind PDF as a one-line nudge
- If no reply by day 14, archive the thread, do not chase a fourth time (per onboarding doctrine: 3 follow-ups then archive)
