# Yarn and Yield - 5 Vision Themes

These are the conceptual bridges between what is on the property today and what we offer. Each theme is one possible centre of gravity for the deck. They are not all-or-nothing - the deck threads several of these.

## Theme 1: The Operating System for a Living Cooperative

**Frame:** Yarn and Yield's three branches (Connection, Knowledge, Practice) are not abstract values. They are operating systems for collective work. Every branch produces events, conversations, learnings, contributions. Today most of that runs in heads, group chats, and a website. As Yarn and Yield grows, that breaks. Co-Exist is the digital substrate for branched cooperative work: branches as collectives, events as gatherings, members as contributors, contributions as visible work.

**Strength:** Speaks Helen's structural language directly. Reframes our platform as alignment with her thesis, not commodity software.

**Weakness:** Can read as systems-encroaching-on-people if delivered wrong. Mitigation: lead with people, place, branches; bring software in only as substrate.

**Use this theme as:** the spine of slide 4 (shared thesis) into slide 6 (Tier 1).

## Theme 2: Land Has a Memory; So Does a Network

**Frame:** Nalbo is named after the Nalbo people who held it. Triunia Reserve next door has cut tree stumps and springboard notches still visible from late-1800s logging - the land remembers. A community that grows around the land also accumulates memory: who turned up, what they planted, what the seasons brought, what the elders said. Most cooperatives lose that memory the moment a founder steps back. Ecodia's wider work - knowledge graphs, persistent institutional memory, AI as long-term steward - is exactly about not losing memory across generations of contributors. Co-Exist's events + impact-stats layer is the simple end of it; the institutional-memory infrastructure is the deeper layer.

**Strength:** Resonant for Helen's regen-systems thinking. Connects the regen-ag thesis (soil remembers, cycles repeat) with cooperative governance (institutions have to remember too).

**Weakness:** Can sound abstract if not landed concretely. Always pair with: "and here is what that looks like at the next working bee."

**Use this theme as:** a closing-remarks moment, or a single-slide vignette near slide 8 (long arc).

## Theme 3: From One Land in Trust to a Network in Trust

**Frame:** Y&Y's stated long-arc is to build a multi-property CLT network across Australia by inviting other landowners to sell their land into the trust. That is a coordination problem before it is a property problem. Properties have to talk to each other, members have to flow between them, capital + work + knowledge have to move with provenance. There is no off-the-shelf software for an Australian multi-property regenerative CLT. We are building something close to it for the Landcare network. The leap from Landcare to CLT is small. We could be the technology partner for that leap, when Yarn and Yield is ready.

**Strength:** Goes directly to the long-arc Helen has stated publicly. Demonstrates we have understood the full ambition, not just the seed.

**Weakness:** Easy to come across as overpromising or ahead of the room. Mitigation: keep it on slide 8 only, named as Tier 3, mentioned once.

**Use this theme as:** Tier 3 / slide 8 only. Do not lead with this.

## Theme 4: Reciprocity is the Only Honest First Move

**Frame:** Most pitches to a regenerative cooperative come from a vendor or an investor. Both extract. We propose to start the relationship by giving rather than asking. One event on Nalbo, in 90 days, run on Co-Exist, with Tate's body on the property doing the work. No invoice. No platform charge. The cost to Yarn and Yield is a yes and a calendar slot. The cost to us is real time and real labour. That is the asymmetry that should set the tone.

**Strength:** This IS the Tier 1 ask, framed in language Helen will recognise. Reciprocity is one of Y&Y's stated values. We are matching their language.

**Weakness:** If we deliver this well, we cannot then walk back to a vendor relationship in Phase 2. That has to be earned through the Tier 1 evidence.

**Use this theme as:** the heart of slide 6 (Tier 1) and the implicit closing argument of slide 10 (asks).

## Theme 5: A Sunshine Coast Pair, not a Sydney Vendor

**Frame:** Most regen-tech in Australia comes out of Sydney or Melbourne. Most of it is run by people who have never been to the Sunshine Coast. Yarn and Yield is a Sunshine Coast project. Ecodia is a Sunshine Coast company. Andrew Maitland walks both worlds. Tate's mum and Alicia met during Easter tree planting at Woodfordia / Lake Gkula. The threads were already crossed before either of us decided to talk. There is something quiet but real about the right local pair finding each other before either of us went looking.

**Strength:** Geographic and relational moat. Not replicable by any out-of-region pitcher.

**Weakness:** None really. This is the easiest theme to deliver and the highest-trust one. Risk is over-leaning on it and skipping the substantive offer.

**Use this theme as:** slide 1 framing + slide 5 (why we are well-positioned) + the implicit close.
