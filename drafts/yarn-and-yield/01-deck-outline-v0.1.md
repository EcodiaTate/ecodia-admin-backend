A. **Neighbours, not vendors.** Ecodia + Yarn and Yield.
B. **A Sunshine Coast hand on the regenerative network.**
C. **Same thesis, different tool. How Ecodia can serve Yarn and Yield.**

I lean A. It does the most positioning work in three words.

---

## The argument the deck must make, in one paragraph

Yarn and Yield is building something we already share the thesis of: regenerative land in collective care, a cooperative of people contributing knowledge / connection / practice, and a long-arc CLT network across Australia. Ecodia is a Sunshine Coast neighbour, vouched for by Andrew Maitland, with Co-Exist - a community-tech platform already running for Landcare across Australia and Victoria - and a Wyoming DAO experiment that puts new economic models into actual practice. We are not asking for capital, land, or members. We are offering presence, working capacity, and platform infrastructure that scales when Yarn and Yield does. Tier 1 is a year of stewardship contributed in kind. Tier 2 and tier 3 follow only if the work earns them.

## Slide-by-slide outline

### Slide 1 - Title + framing

- Title (one of the three above)
- Subtitle: "A proposal from a Sunshine Coast neighbour"
- Names + roles: Tate Donohoe (Authorized Human Representative), EcodiaOS (the AI member of Ecodia DAO LLC, Wyoming)
- One line: "We are here because Andrew told us about you, and because what you are building is what we are also building, from a different angle."

### Slide 2 - What we already know about you (homework on display)

This slide is the trust establisher. It signals: we did our work, we are not generic, we listened.

- Yarn and Yield is a Community Land Trust + Cooperative dual-entity, seeded by Helen and her husband through the purchase of Nalbo at West Woombye
- Three branches: Connection, Knowledge, Practice
- Sunshine Syntropics is the regen-ag arm
- Adjacent to Triunia Environmental Reserve - a wildlife corridor matters
- Long arc: invite other landowners into the CLT to build a multi-property regenerative network across Australia
- One quote from Helen, attributed: "Land should belong to the community, and the community should belong to the land"

### Slide 3 - Who we are

A short, dense, no-fluff intro. Lead with the work, end with the structural novelty.

- Ecodia Pty Ltd - software studio, Sunshine Coast
- Co-Exist - our community-tech platform for conservation cooperatives, currently being deployed for Landcare on the Sunshine Coast and rolling into a Victoria property (Merri Mornings)
- Roam - travel app
- Behind these: Ecodia DAO LLC (Wyoming, formed Apr 2026), with EcodiaOS as 100% sole member (algorithmic manager under W.S. 17-31-104), Tate as Authorized Human Representative. We are running our own experiment in regenerative governance and new economic models. Helen, this is one of the topic areas you speak about. We are doing it.

### Slide 4 - Why we share your thesis

Map their stated values onto ours, briefly. Three columns or three rows. Their value -> our practice.

- "Surpluses stay local. Prosperity is shared, not extracted." -> We operate on a small VPS on the Sunshine Coast. Most of our infrastructure is local. We are not extracting back to a US tech parent.
- "Land in collective care" -> We do not own land. We propose to be in service of land that is. Co-Exist runs as the operating layer for organisations that hold land in trust, like Landcare groups.
- "Cooperative ensures everyone who contributes - knowledge, labour, skills, capital - has a real stake and a voice" -> Our DAO structure is the digital-native version of this thesis. We have built it for ourselves; we know how hard it is.

### Slide 5 - Why we are well-positioned (the unique part)

This is where the deck earns the right to a second slide of asks. Five short bullets, each one a fact only we have.

- We are literal Sunshine Coast neighbours
- Andrew Maitland anchors us inside this room
- The Woodfordia / Conservatree thread already runs through both of us (Easter tree planting, where Tate's mum and Alicia met)
- Co-Exist is multi-tenant and already deployed; when Yarn and Yield scales to multiple properties, the platform problem you will hit is the platform problem we have already solved for Landcare
- We do not need your capital. This is not a fundraising ask in disguise

### Slide 6 - What we propose (Tier 1)

The actual ask. Make it small, concrete, immediate, free.

**Tier 1 - the next 90 days. No invoice. No platform charge.**

- Tate joins as a member-collaborator, contributes to working bees in person
- We run one event on Nalbo within 90 days that uses Co-Exist as the coordination layer (a working bee or tree planting day, signups + check-in + impact-stats + post-event sharing). At the end of it, you have data, members on platform, and a working demo.
- We document what worked, what did not, and what Yarn and Yield could do with it next.

The point of Tier 1: we earn the right to the next conversation. No ongoing commitment from you.

### Slide 7 - What might come next (Tier 2 - mentioned, not pitched)

Show we have thought past the first ask, but make clear it is not on the table today.

- IF Tier 1 lands well, we can talk about Co-Exist as the operating layer for Yarn and Yield: members, branches (Connection / Knowledge / Practice), events, contributions, working bees, capital flows. Per-property pricing, scales as you scale. We are doing this for Landcare; the structure transfers.
- We are not asking you to commit to this today. It is on the page so you know we have a road past Tier 1.

### Slide 8 - The long arc (Tier 3 - hinted at, only if Helen opens the door)

- Yarn and Yield's stated long-arc is multi-property CLT across Australia. The technology + governance layer for that network is not built yet.
- Ecodia is building it for ourselves and for the conservation orgs we work with. There is a credible alignment between your network ambition and our infrastructure direction.
- If you ever decide you want a technology + AI partner for the multi-property CLT, we would be the first conversation.
- This is a 5-10 year horizon. We mention it once.

### Slide 9 - Risks we acknowledge

Pre-empt the obvious objections. Saying them first earns trust.

- "We have a software engineer on the board already." Yes. We are not pitching a custom build. Co-Exist is our platform you would use; your SE keeps building whatever sits on top or alongside.
- "We do not yet have a public face for Yarn and Yield's tech." We respect that. Tier 1 has nothing public-facing - it is one event, internal, low-risk for you.
- "Why not pay us to do it." Because reciprocity is more honest than vendor-client at this stage. If we are still doing this in a year, then we both have evidence to set commercial terms.

### Slide 10 - Asks of you, today

Three concrete requests, no more.

1. A yes / not-yet on Tier 1 (one event, 90 days, no invoice).
2. An introduction to whichever board member runs the events / branches calendar so we can scope the first working bee jointly.
3. A second meeting in 30 days to compare notes after the event runs.

That is the deck.
