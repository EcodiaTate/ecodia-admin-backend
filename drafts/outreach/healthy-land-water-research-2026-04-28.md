## Organisation context

Healthy Land and Water is the **NRM regional body for South East Queensland**. Member-based not-for-profit. Catchment area covers from north of Noosa to south of Beaudesert, including:
- 18 catchments
- 5 Moreton Bay zones
- The Sunshine Coast (Maroochy catchment specifically) - Tate's geography
- Brisbane River, Pine River, Caboolture River, Logan-Albert system

**HLW Report Card 2025 is the milestone 25-year edition** (October 2025 release). Marks the longest-running waterway-and-environment health monitoring program in Australia. This is the headline moment in their public communications calendar for 2025-2026.

## What the 2025 Report Card actually says

Two sets of indicators: environmental condition + social/economic.

**Maroochy catchment (Tate's local):**
- Environmental condition: "Fair"
- Social and economic: "Very high"
- Maroochy estuary: high performer in fish monitoring, vast seagrass meadows, healthy fish populations.

**Region-wide pressures flagged in 2025:**
- Nitrogen levels rising in Brisbane, Pine and Caboolture estuaries (above target for stable estuarine ecology)
- 3,767 hectares of riparian woody vegetation lost 2018-2023
- Of that, 387 ha was remnant native forest (i.e. permanent loss of mature ecosystem)
- Riparian degradation = direct upstream cause of declining estuary health

**Implication for outreach framing:** HLW is publicly committed to a multi-decade dataset and has freshly published the 2025 milestone. Their problem statement for the next decade is **how do you turn 25 years of monitoring into measurable, fundable on-ground outcomes** - which is the exact gap a sub-commercial-scale MRV platform addresses.
