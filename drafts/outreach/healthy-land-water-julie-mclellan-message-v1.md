I'm Tate Donohoe, founder of Ecodia, a Sunshine Coast software studio that builds infrastructure for conservation peak bodies. The question I keep coming back to is how that dataset converts into the next decade of measurable, fundable on-ground outcomes, particularly for the long tail of sub-commercial-scale projects in catchments like Maroochy, where the Report Card shows the environmental rating sitting at "fair" but social and economic value rated "very high". That gap is the exact problem we have been building software for: a dMRV layer that wraps CER-blessed methods (FullCAM, CFI-MER-2018, Nature Repair Market schemas) so a regional body can run their own member-led carbon and biodiversity projects without giving 30% of credit value to an external aggregator. Software subscription, retained IP, no revenue share.

We have a lighthouse deployment in the SEQ region with another peak body and a working technical spec. Rather than send a deck cold, I would value 30 minutes of your time to understand where HLW sees the bottleneck between the Report Card data and project-scale outcomes, and to share what we have learned about the sub-commercial-scale gap. Happy to come to the Brisbane office, or dial in.

If the timing is wrong, no obligation. I would still appreciate knowing whether this is a conversation for HLW or a conversation for someone else in your network.
