## Operational pain signals (publicly visible)

1. **Co-op office is open Tuesdays and Thursdays 8:30am–12:30pm only.** Eight hours of public-facing admin coverage per week for a 240-resident village with a busy events calendar (markets, courses, weddings, conferences). Operations bottleneck.
2. **Website is hand-maintained by one resident (Bruce Gibson).** Visual style is mid-2010s. Booking flow appears to be manual back-and-forth via the Operations Manager rather than an integrated platform.
3. **Course pricing is precise but the booking funnel is informal.** August 2026 PDC: 18 spots, $2,250 early-bird, $2,450 standard, deposit $250, accommodation extra $250–$420. No public sense of how many spots are filled, no live availability, no automated payment flow visible.
4. **No public board/management committee roster.** I could not surface a current Co-op chair or Body Corporate chair from public sources. This is a normal small-cooperative pattern but suggests the Co-op runs on personal trust rather than a published governance ledger.

I deliberately did not search forums or member-only spaces for governance disputes. Those are private and reading them would be a trust violation. Public coverage shows none.
