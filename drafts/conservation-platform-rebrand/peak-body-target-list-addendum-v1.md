## Why this addendum exists

Three fork research passes ran overnight against the assumptions in v1. All three completed. Their findings are concrete enough to change the recommended first-outreach target, tighten the wedge urgency, and surface one channel-adjacent threat the v1 deck does not currently acknowledge. Folding them in.

The three findings, each with a one-sentence verdict:

1. **Wedge defensibility (Research 3137): CONFIRMED-OPEN, but window tightened from 12 months to 9-12 months.** Cecil Earth, AgriProve, Loam Bio confirmed zero peak-body or NRM-regional-body customers. Cecil's actual customer list is corporate carbon project developers (Corporate Carbon, Forest Carbon, Impact Ag Partners, Respira, Treevive, et al.) and AgriProve's is 18 individual farmer projects + MLA (industry levy body). None of them are positioned to capture the federation-of-aggregators tier we are aiming at.
2. **International competitor AU footprint (Research 3142): wedge holds on channel, but Downforce Technologies has material AU footprint (NAB, Asahi, Coles, Sundown Pastoral, Carbon Neutral Group, Charles Sturt University via Boolah Group's Regen4real, Head of APAC Ben Wark, CEFC backed).** Downforce competes in finance + corporate-supply-chain channels, NOT in the peak-body / NRM-regional-body channel. Carbonfuture has one AU project (Mardie biochar WA, exclusive). Pachama, Carbon Direct, SustainCERT have zero AU presence. Bundle deck v2 needs minor revision to acknowledge Downforce and differentiate on channel rather than ignore them.
3. **Decision-maker map (Research 3144): recommended first-outreach target shifts to Julie McLellan, CEO Healthy Land & Water (HLW)** with two concrete shared-context points (HLW's annual SEQ Report Card 2025 + Tate's Sunshine Coast geography sitting inside HLW's catchment). Tess Herbert is the highest-value federation bridge (Statewide NSW LLS Board Chair AND NRM Regions Australia board director - simultaneous influence over 11 NSW LLS regions and federation endorsement). Dr Mat Hardy is the on-thesis project lead at NRM Regions Australia (Environmental Markets Participation Initiative).

The rest of this addendum spells out what changes in v1 as a result.

## What changes

### 1. Wedge urgency tightens

v1 line ("AgriProve and GreenCollar do not serve them because the per-project economics are too thin for revenue-share") stands. The supplementary claim - that the wedge has a 12-month window - revises down to **9-12 months** based on the following observations:

- Cecil Earth raised AUD 6.5M in mid-2024 and has since shipped a large language model copilot (Tom) and signed Forest Carbon as their largest UK deployment. Their roadmap velocity suggests an Australian peak-body product is plausible inside 12 months if they decide to ship one.
- AgriProve sits inside the Macdoch Group and is reported to be considering category expansion. They have the soil-ACCU project pipeline that would make a peak-body aggregator pivot economically rational.
- Loam Bio is biotech-anchored and unlikely to enter the peak-body channel directly, but their SecondCrop pilot model is the template a peak-body adjacent provider could lift.

The implication for the approach sequence: the three-tier sequencing in v1 still holds, but the gap between Landcare and NRM Regions Australia compresses from "approximately three weeks" to "approximately two weeks", and the gap between NRM Regions and ICIN compresses from "approximately two weeks" to "10-14 days". Total Tier 1 cycle time targets ~6 weeks rather than ~8 weeks.

### 2. Recommended first-outreach target update

v1 ordered Tier 1 as Landcare → NRM Regions → ICIN → AbCF. That order still holds for **federation-level** outreach. Research 3144 surfaced a higher-conviction **regional-NRM-body** first-outreach target that should run in parallel with the Landcare federation conversation:

**Julie McLellan, CEO, Healthy Land & Water (julie@hlw.org.au).**

Why her, why now, why HLW:

- HLW is the regional NRM body for South East Queensland - covers the Sunshine Coast catchment where Tate lives and where the lighthouse deployment runs. The geographic-shared-context hook is concrete and verifiable in one screen of a satellite map.
- HLW publishes the SEQ Report Card every year. The 2025 edition is current. The Report Card is exactly the operational-evidence-and-public-reporting workflow that the platform's operational sync layer is built for.
- Julie McLellan has been CEO since 2013 and is one of the longest-tenured regional NRM body CEOs in Australia. Tenure means relationships into the federation (NRM Regions Australia) and into the Queensland state network. A successful HLW conversation gives both peak-body and regional-NRM bona fides in one go.
- The HLW chair is Stephen Robertson (former Queensland Minister for Natural Resources and Mines). Government and policy fluency at the board level reduces procurement-novelty risk on the AI-LLC structure conversation.

The framing for the HLW conversation (when Tate green-lights):

> Two specific shared-context points - we operate from inside your catchment (Sunshine Coast), and we have a software platform that produces the operational evidence backbone for the kind of public reporting your annual SEQ Report Card runs on. The platform is in production at a charity in your demographic. We are not at the stage of selling, we are at the stage of asking whether the operational complexity of the SEQ Report Card workflow is something HLW would benefit from a tool for.

This conversation runs in parallel with Landcare. It is not a substitute for the federation-track. It is a regional-NRM proof point that strengthens the federation conversation. If both land, the NRM Regions Australia conversation arrives with two warm references.

### 3. Federation-bridge target

**Tess Herbert** is the highest-value single contact in the entire decision-maker map. She holds two simultaneous board roles:

- Statewide Chair of NSW Local Land Services (the NSW state government body that coordinates the 11 LLS regions across NSW)
- Board Director of NRM Regions Australia (the national peak body)

A single warm-intro to Tess gives access to NRM-Regions-AU board endorsement AND the 11 NSW LLS regions in one move. She is the federation-level analogue of what Kurt is for the Co-Exist board: a single relationship with a multiplied reach. Worth 2-3x the effort to find a warm path to her over any other federation-tier contact.

Tess goes into the target list as a new entry under Tier 1, after Landcare and before NRM Regions Australia in priority. The approach is to find the warm path; the conversation itself runs after the Landcare conversation has produced a reference.

### 4. Incoming-CEO timing window

**Marnie Lassen commences as CEO of NRM Regions Australia in early May 2026.** Within ~10 days of this addendum being written.

Incoming CEOs run discovery conversations in their first 60-90 days. The window for a discovery-fit conversation with Marnie sits between roughly mid-May and end-July 2026. Outside that window, peak-body CEO calendars consolidate into delivery work and discovery conversations become harder to land.

Operational consequence: the NRM Regions Australia approach should be ready to fire by mid-May. Materials for that approach include a one-page brief on the platform, a one-page brief on the lighthouse deployment in production, a one-page on the AI-LLC substrate disclosure, and a single concrete proposal (showcase slot in the carbon and environmental markets discussion group, or briefing slot at an upcoming Community of Practice meeting).

### 8. Bundle deck v2 minor revision needed

Research 3142 found Downforce Technologies has material AU presence:

- NAB (banking partnership for soil carbon finance products)
- Asahi (corporate buyer)
- Coles (corporate buyer)
- Sundown Pastoral (operator-side)
- Carbon Neutral Group (buyer-side)
- Charles Sturt University via Boolah Group's Regen4real (academic+operator)
- Ben Wark, Head of APAC, based in Australia
- CEFC (Clean Energy Finance Corporation) backing

Downforce is in the **finance and corporate-supply-chain channel**. They sell to banks for green-loan products and to corporate buyers for supply-chain decarbonisation reporting. They do NOT sell to peak bodies, NRM regional bodies, or community-aggregator NFPs.

The wedge holds. But the v2 deck currently positions against US/UK competitors as "no AU presence". That claim is now incorrect and gets disconfirmed in 30 seconds by anyone in the conservation-finance space who knows the Downforce-NAB partnership. Minor revision: acknowledge Downforce explicitly, differentiate on channel, do not soften the wedge claim.

Suggested deck v2 wording (one slide or one paragraph, per the deck's existing style):

> Downforce Technologies has Australian footprint (NAB, Asahi, Coles, Sundown Pastoral, CEFC backed). They sell to banks and corporate-supply-chain buyers. Our channel is the peak-body, NRM-regional-body, and Indigenous-aggregator tier - the federation shape that delivers the projects on the ground rather than the finance and corporate procurement layer that buys the certificates downstream. The two channels are economically and operationally distinct; both can win.

This positions us as not-naive about the AU competitive landscape and reinforces the wedge thesis on its actual basis (channel) rather than on a false absence claim.

### 9. Carbonfuture, Pachama, Carbon Direct, SustainCERT - confirmed non-presence

For the record, Research 3142 confirmed:

- **Pachama**: Acquired by Carbon Direct in 2024. Zero AU presence post-acquisition.
- **Carbon Direct**: Zero AU presence in the carbon-MRV-software layer.
- **SustainCERT**: Zero AU presence.

These are background context for the deck. The channel argument rests on Downforce; the rest are not present in the AU market in any way that affects the peak-body wedge.
