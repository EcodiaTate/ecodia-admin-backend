## 1. Organisational profile

**Legal entity / form.** NRM Regions Australia is the **federal peak body for Australia's 54 regional Natural Resource Management organisations** [source: `~/ecodiaos/research/carbon-mrv/nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27.md` Section 1; `peak-body-target-list-v1.md` entry 2]. The body is constituted as a member-organisation peak with a board of nine (one director from each of the eight jurisdictions plus an independent chair) [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Section 6; the count of nine is stated; named directors are not publicly aggregated in our internal data beyond Tess Herbert].

**Member organisation count.** **54 operating member bodies** corresponding to **56 spatially defined NRM regions** under the bilateral agreements signed between the Commonwealth and each State / Territory between 2001 and 2004; some adjacent regions share a single delivery body, which is where the 56 vs 54 discrepancy lives [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Section 1].

**Headcount.** Inferred fewer than ten full-time equivalents. Public-domain detail on operating revenue and staff count for FY2023-24 is thin in our indexed source set; the FY2023-24 annual report PDF (https://nrmregionsaustralia.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/NRM-RA-Annual-Report-2024.pdf) would carry the figures and is `[unsourced - verify]` from inside this fork because WebFetch was blocked [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Section 6].

**Funding model (peak-body level).** Member contributions plus Commonwealth grants for sector-coordination work. Operating budget inferred to sit in the low millions of AUD annually [source: same Section 6, explicitly inferred not evidential].

**Mission and operating mode.** The peak body is principally a coordinating body. It does NOT procure software on behalf of its members. It influences sector-wide procurement only through advocacy, common-platform proposals, and informal coordination at the CEO Forum [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Section 6]. **This is the single most operationally consequential fact in the dossier and it directly shapes the recommended next move below.**

**Programs we have line-of-sight on (internal evidence).**
- **Nature-based Solutions Community of Practice** [source: peak-body-target-list-v1.md entry 2; peak-body-target-list-v2.md entry 3]
- **Carbon and environmental markets discussion group**, meets every 6-8 weeks across the 54 members [source: same]
- **Environmental Markets Participation Initiative (EMPI)**, federally funded by DCCEEW, co-delivered with NRM Regions Australia. Stage 1 (~2023-2024) capability and learning. Stage 2 (~2024-2026, current) extension, outreach, project-development support [source: `nrm-regions-au-research-2026-04-28.md`]
- **Carbon + Biodiversity Pilot** federal program under the $34M Agriculture Stewardship Package, six member regional NRM bodies enrolled (Burnett-Mary QLD, Central West NSW, North Central VIC, NRM North TAS, Eyre Peninsula SA, South West WA) [source: peak-body-target-list-v1.md entry 2]

## 2. Strategic context: why they matter to the conservation-platform-rebrand thesis

**The federation thesis.** NRM Regions Australia is the **only addressable single-conversation surface that touches all 54 regional NRM bodies**. Per the Mat Hardy depth-prep dossier, Julie McLellan represents one region (HLW SEQ), Tess Herbert represents 11 regions (NSW LLS) plus a federation board seat, and an EMPI-level program lead can have federation-level operational reach into all 54 via EMPI participation [source: `nrm-regions-au-research-2026-04-28.md`].

**The realised commercial role: marketing channel, not purchasing channel.** Per the NRM procurement intelligence research, federation through NRM Regions Australia is a marketing channel rather than a purchasing channel. Peak-body endorsement de-risks regional sales by an order of magnitude. Closing the peak-body relationship costs 6-12 months of relationship work and yields zero direct revenue. The ROI is downstream regional close-rate and average-deal-size lift [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Sections 6 and 8; Neo4j Research "NRM body procurement intelligence 2026-04-27 (carbon-MRV GTM extension)"].

**Where our offering overlaps.**
- The peak-body-cooperative-MRV thesis (peak operates a per-tenant dMRV layer, members pool submission, fixed costs amortise across the network) is a direct technical answer to the small-project participation problem EMPI Stage 2 outreach is trying to surface.
- The platform's federation shape (peak body to regional bodies to local groups) maps cleanly to the 54-member federation shape NRM Regions Australia coordinates.
- The Carbon and Environmental Markets Discussion Group is a natural showcase venue for the dMRV add-on demonstration; not a procurement venue.

**Where our offering does NOT overlap (gaps surfaced by deepening this dossier).**
- **MERIT, FieldCapture, BioCollect.** All three are ALA / DCCEEW federal common assets, free at point of use to grant recipients, and mandatory for DCCEEW-funded reporting [source: nrm-procurement-processes-2026-04-27 Section 4]. Any pitch into the federation must position the platform as **complementary to MERIT** (operational evidence backbone that feeds MERIT submission), NOT as a replacement. Failure to make this distinction killed the conversation before it started in adjacent sectors per the procurement-intelligence research.
- **Whole-of-government ESRI and Microsoft EAs.** New direct ESRI / Microsoft commercial sales to regional NRM bodies are uncommon because state-government enterprise agreements already cover them. Our platform must avoid encroaching on the GIS / collaboration tier or, if it does, must be priced and positioned as an SME-direct purchase rather than a panel-tendered enterprise tool.
- **Carbon and MRV-specific tooling at the regional level is largely absent today.** Where regions are involved in carbon-method projects, the MRV stack is procured by the project developer or aggregator, rarely by the NRM body itself [source: same Section 4]. **This is a confirmed open wedge but it is also an empty channel by default - the regional body is not the buyer for ACCU-method MRV; the project developer is.** A federation-level conversation must therefore frame our dMRV layer as cooperative-aggregator-owned (peak-body-operated for member benefit) rather than project-developer-owned, otherwise the channel does not match the procurement reality.

**The strategic question the federation conversation must answer (NOT yet resolved internally).** When EMPI Stage 2 outreach moves landholders from "we understand environmental markets exist" to "we have a project pipeline with measurable participation", who holds the dMRV contract for that pipeline? Three plausible buyers (regional NRM body acting as cooperative aggregator on member behalf; specialist Indigenous aggregator like ICIN / AbCF; private project developer like AgriProve / GreenCollar / Cecil Earth). Our wedge thesis says the first. The federation conversation is partly about whether NRM Regions Australia agrees with that framing. **Open question, see Section 5 below.**
