02  /  The pattern

A peak-body federation runs on spreadsheets, email, and WhatsApp groups across 5 to 300 sub-orgs.

State-level Landcare networks. Conservation Volunteers Australia. Regional NRM bodies. Junior Landcare's 12,000+ schools and groups on the National Landcare Directory. Coastcare, Bushcare, Rivercare. Trust-for-Nature covenantor cohorts.

Same shape every time. People show up. They organise into local groups. They run events on the ground. They record what happened. The result has to land somewhere a funder, a board, and a regulator can read.

The infrastructure layer underneath this is missing. The work is held together by individual volunteers carrying the seams between four or five separate tools. When a coordinator changes role, the institutional memory walks out with the laptop.

The 6,000 Landcare groups, 100,000 to 140,000 volunteers, and 12,000 Junior Landcare schools quoted in Landcare Australia's 2023/2024 Annual Report describe a federation problem with no current platform answer.

07  /  Carbon revenue add-on

Members operate their own MRV. Peak body aggregates submission. Revenue stays inside the federation.

A peak-body member today has two paths into ACCU revenue. Either skip carbon credits because the project is too small to interest a national project developer, or sign over a 30% revenue share to one to get to scale. Neither path keeps the money inside the network of stewards who actually do the work.

The market is real and growing. The Clean Energy Regulator issued 19.5 million ACCUs in 2025 and the published trajectory points to 33.3 million by 2034, a compound annual growth rate of 5.93%. AgriProve currently runs roughly 70% of soil-carbon project starts. GreenCollar's standard aggregator share sits at around 30% of revenue. Both economically need broadacre projects above $100k aggregate-ACCU value to make the project-developer model work. The peak-body steward running 50 to 300 hectares falls through the floor.

The flip is structural. The peak body operates a software-only digital MRV layer for its members, and aggregates submission to the Clean Energy Regulator on their behalf. Ecodia provides the platform under a SaaS licence and a flat per-project issuance fee. Ecodia takes no share of the credit revenue.

  • EOSDA satellite layer for soil organic carbon, integrated as a Capacitor data-source on the same Supabase substrate that already runs reverse-Excel-sync. Vegetation and savanna methodologies layer on the same source.
  • CSV soil-test ingestion with GPS metadata, on the existing 30-minute Edge Function cron pattern. Lab results land in the field record they came from.
  • CER submission templates pre-wired to the soil-method (CFI-MER-2018) and other Emissions Reduction Fund methodologies. Submission pack assembled per-project, signed off by the peak body.
  • Bi-temporal audit trail, graph plus relational. Every reading, correction, and re-submission is traceable to both the field event and the wall-clock event. Auditor reads the timeline; member keeps the data.
  • Per-project issuance fee inside the licence. No share of the credit revenue. The economic alignment is software-as-infrastructure, paid for as infrastructure.
